# Supplementary Figure Legends

**Supplementary Figure S1 | STEREO per-behavior classification performance.** (**A**) Confusion matrix between two independent human observers (H1 x H2), expressed as percentages relative to Observer 1 labels. Behavior categories are indicated on both axes. (**B**) Confusion matrix between Observer 1 and the STEREO classifier (H1 x STEREO). (**C**) Per-behavior F1 scores for H1 x H2 (purple) and H1 x STEREO (red). Dashed lines indicate macro-average F1. (**D**–**E**) Per-behavior precision and recall for both comparisons. The STEREO classifier achieves comparable agreement to inter-rater reliability for most behaviors, with the lowest F1 for grooming (0.73) due to confusion with the undefined category.

**Supplementary Figure S2 | Development of all nine behaviors across repeated cocaine sessions.** (**A**) Mean fraction of time spent in each behavior across saline (S1-S3) and cocaine (C1-C5) sessions (N = 24 mice from 4 cohorts). Error bars represent SEM. (**B**) Bout duration CDFs for five key behaviors (floor licking, wall licking, grooming, locomotion, rearing), comparing early (cocaine 1, orange) and late (cocaine 5, dark red) cocaine sessions. (**C**) Behavioral dynamics metrics: mean transition rate (switches/min), number of distinct behaviors per 3-min window, and switch entropy time course across the 20-min session for each trial. Shading represents SEM. ***P < 0.001; **P < 0.01; *P < 0.05; n.s. = not significant.

**Supplementary Figure S3 | iSPN optogenetic stimulation alters behavioral transition structure.** (**A**-**C**) Mean transition probability matrices during laser-OFF (**A**), laser-ON (**B**), and their difference (ON - OFF; **C**) periods across all stimulation epochs. Rows represent the origin behavior and columns represent the destination behavior. In panel C, asterisks mark transitions significantly altered by laser stimulation (paired t-test, P < 0.05). (**D**) V-graphs showing the fraction of time spent in surface licking, locomotion, and grooming across laser-OFF, laser-ON, and recovery bins. Gray lines represent individual mice; colored points represent mean +/- SEM (n = 7 mice). ***P < 0.001; **P < 0.01; *P < 0.05; n.s. = not significant. Error bars and shading represent SEM.

**Supplementary Figure S4 | dSPN optogenetic stimulation drives rigid behavioral transitions and prolonged bouts.** (**A**-**C**) Mean behavioral transition probability matrices during laser-OFF (**A**), laser-ON (**B**), and their difference (ON - OFF; **C**) for dSPN-stimulated mice (n = 11). Asterisks in panel C indicate significant changes (paired t-test, P < 0.05). (**D**-**H**) Cumulative distribution functions of bout durations for grooming, body licking, wall licking, floor licking, and locomotion during laser-ON (purple) versus laser-OFF (gray) periods. KS = Kolmogorov-Smirnov test statistic. ***P < 0.001; **P < 0.01; *P < 0.05; n.s. = not significant.

**Supplementary Figure S5 | Bidirectional DREADDs effects across all five cohorts.** Each row corresponds to one cohort: Drd1-hm4Di, Drd1-hm3Dq, controls, A2a-hm4Di, and A2a-hm3Dq. Columns show: (1) Grouped V-graphs for surface licking, self-directed licking, and non-licking fractions across Pre-CNO, During-CNO, and Post-CNO sessions; (2-4) Individual behavior V-graphs for floor licking, wall licking, and locomotion. Gray lines represent individual mice; colored lines represent group means +/- SEM. Mouse counts per cohort are indicated in row titles. The sandwich design (Pre-CNO -> CNO -> Post-CNO) provides within-subject controls. ***P < 0.001; **P < 0.01; *P < 0.05; n.s. = not significant. Error bars represent SEM. See Table S1 for detailed statistics.

**Supplementary Figure S6 | Stacked behavioral distributions across and within sessions.** (**A**) Stacked bar plot showing the full behavioral distribution (all 9 behaviors) for each session (S1-S3, C1-C5). **Supplementary Figure S7 | Behavioral entropy and transition dynamics during optogenetic and chemogenetic manipulations.** (**A**-**B**) Switch entropy (**A**) and behavioral transition rate (**B**) during laser-ON versus laser-OFF epochs for iSPN (A2a) and dSPN (Drd1) optogenetic stimulation. (**C**-**D**) Same metrics across DREADDs sandwich conditions (Pre-CNO, During-CNO, Post-CNO) for all five cohorts. Individual data points shown with group mean +/- SEM. ***P < 0.001; **P < 0.01; *P < 0.05; n.s. = not significant (paired t-test for opto; paired t-test for DREADDs). Error bars represent SEM.

**Supplementary Figure S8 | dSPN versus iSPN bout durations during cocaine exposure.** (**A**-**B**) Bout duration CDFs for floor licking (**A**) and wall licking (**B**) in dSPN-recorded (red) and iSPN-recorded (dark red) mice during the cocaine 5 session. KS = Kolmogorov-Smirnov test comparing distributions between pathways. This analysis addresses whether differences in photometry peak amplitudes between pathways could be explained by systematic differences in bout duration. ***P < 0.001; **P < 0.01; *P < 0.05; n.s. = not significant.

**Supplementary Figure S9 | Context specificity of cocaine-induced behavioral changes.** (**A**) Fold change in time spent per behavior during the salineOnly session (post-cocaine, cocaine-paired context, no drug) relative to saline baseline (mean of saline 1-3). Dashed line at 1.0 indicates no change. Horizontal bars are colored by behavior; asterisks mark significant differences (paired t-test). (**B**) Paired comparison of surface licking fraction between saline baseline and salineOnly (n = 14 mice). Gray lines represent individual mice. (**C**) Paired comparison of normalized behavioral entropy. ***P < 0.001; **P < 0.01; *P < 0.05; n.s. = not significant. Error bars represent SEM.

**Supplementary Figure S10 | Velocity during surface licking confirms stationary classification.** (**A**-**B**) Cumulative distribution functions of instantaneous velocity during floor licking (**A**) and wall licking (**B**) frames for each session (saline 1-3 and cocaine 1-5). Colors follow the cocaine escalation gradient. (**C**) Fraction of surface licking frames below 0.5 cm/s across sessions (N = 24 mice). Dashed line indicates fully stationary threshold. (**D**) Mean velocity comparison between floor licking and wall licking across sessions. This confirms that surface licking is performed while functionally stationary (<0.5 cm/s in >75% of frames). Error bars represent SEM.
